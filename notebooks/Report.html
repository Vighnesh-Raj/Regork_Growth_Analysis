Introduction

Regork's CEO is faced with declining customer engagement and revenue performance. Addressing these issues is critical for improving profitability and maintaining market competitiveness.

Problem Statement: How can the grocery chain effectively identify low-performing products that generate minimal sales among repeat customers and strategically invest in those products to increase overall revenue and profitability?

Methodology: We analyzed customer transaction data using exploratory data analysis (EDA) and machine learning techniques to identify patterns, segment customers, and optimize marketing strategies.

Solution Overview: The Complete Journey dataset offers detailed household-level transaction data collected over 2017 from 2,469 frequent grocery store shoppers. By leveraging data insights, we propose which product types have untapped potential, analyzing purchasing patterns of repeat customers, and implementing targeted promotional strategies or product enhancements to drive increased customer engagement and higher sales for these underperforming categories.

# Convert product_id to a consistent type (string) in all DataFrames
top_50_repeating_products['product_id'] = top_50_repeating_products['product_id'].astype(str)
coupons['product_id'] = coupons['product_id'].astype(str)
promotions['product_id'] = promotions['product_id'].astype(str)

# Step 1: Merge top_50_repeating_products with coupons
coupon_dep = top_50_repeating_products.merge(coupons, how='inner', on='product_id')

# Step 2: Add display_location by merging with promotions
coupon_dep = coupon_dep.merge(promotions[['product_id', 'display_location']], how='left', on='product_id')

# Step 3: Drop rows with missing values for relevant columns
coupon_dep = coupon_dep.dropna(subset=['department', 'product_category', 'coupon_upc', 'display_location'])

# Step 4: Exclude rows where display_location equals 0
coupon_dep = coupon_dep[coupon_dep['display_location'] != '0']

# Step 5: Group by department, product category, and display location
coupon_dep1 = (
    coupon_dep.groupby(['department', 'product_category', 'display_location'])['coupon_upc']
    .count()
    .reset_index()
    .rename(columns={'coupon_upc': 'coupon_count'})
)

# Step 6: Pivot data for plotting
group_data = coupon_dep1.pivot_table(
    index=['department', 'display_location'],

    values='coupon_count',

Summary

Recommendations for CEO

  • Analysis of repeating customers show that middle-income households are a key customer base, and promotions targeting this demographic may yield higher retention and spending.

  • Targeting the 35-54 age group with relevant products and services could boost revenue further. Outreach to younger demographics can capture future repeat customers.

  • The drug line cater to niche demands, efforts could be made to attract a broader customer base or better target the niche audience through personalized marketing campaigns.

  • Offer more coupons on departments such as Drugs and Produce to maximize revenue from redemptions

  • Strategic Placement of Products: grocery products in the Front-End Cap of stores, Drugs in the Side Aisle and Produce towards the store front

Limitations

  • Collect more data for remaining households to better understand the demographics of the customers of the grocery chain
  • Collect newer data. Since this data is from 2017, it is hard to accurately predict trends in 2024. Including more recent transaction history so it represent the customer behavior of recent time.
  • Inclusion of Product Brand Names can help classify product types better. Some brands might offer coupons different than others of the same product type but it cannot be represented accurately without brand data
